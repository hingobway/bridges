Golden Gate Bridge

The Golden Gate Bridge is an iconic example of a suspension bridge in action. Let’s take a look at this bridge and its impressive supporting cables.

Finding a Catenary Equation

Based on the forces proof earlier, we know we’re looking for some form of positive quadratic curve. We can’t find the actual forces on the bridge, because linear density and perpendicular cable tension require knowledge of the materials that is unattainable. However, using points and statistics about the bridge, we can get a quite good estimate.

Measurement Distance (m)
Bridge Length (between support pillars) 1280
Tower Height (roadway to top) 152
Tower Height water to roadway) 75
Distance between supports (cable to deck) 15.24
Total cable length (goal) 2332
Source: goldengatebridge.org
